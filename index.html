Welcome!

Hi, I am a Ph.D. candidate and research associate in the Collaborative Artificial Intelligence (CAI) group under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Andreas Bulling at the University of Stuttgart, Germany and a Ph.D. scholar at the International Max Planck Research School for Intelligent Systems (IMPRS-IS). Next to Andreas Bulling, Prof. Dr. Georg Martius (Max Planck Institute for Intelliegent Systems, University of Tübingen) and Dr. Charley Wu (University of Tübingen) are part of my IMPRS-IS Thesis Advisory Committee. My work aims to improve human-AI cooperation by investigating the intersection of several different research fields, including MARL, Computational Theory of Mind, Zero-Shot Cooperation and Open-endedness:
  1. I reasearch the intersection between partner and map generalisation, i.e. in The Overcooked Generalisation Challenge.
  2. I research how computational Theory of Mind and Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning can be combined to improve zero-shot human-AI cooperation.
  3. I try to adress how open-ended learning is connected to learning Theory of Mind in artificial agents.
You can find more about me or my work on the about page, on our groups website, or on google scholar. For students interested in a project or thesis please check the open thesis page.
